Why 150, not 100: Your broadcast channel alone has 700 opted-in members. If just 10% subscribe to YouTube, that's 70 from one channel before you've touched Facebook or the forum. Add the rest of your platforms and 100 is already half-earned. Aim for 150 — treat 100 as the floor.
What could push you past 200: One piece of content escaping your existing audience — a funny take getting screenshot-shared in a footy group chat, someone with a bigger following reposting your story, or a Reel getting picked up by the algorithm. Make the content genuinely fun and shareable, not just promotional.
Watch out for: People holding their take until the live. That's fine for viewer numbers — but you won't see a surge in YouTube activity until Tuesday. Sub growth happens across the 4 days; engagement peaks on the night.
"Best take wins" is the right mechanic. A random draw gives you subs but low engagement. A judged take gives people a reason to watch the live, fills your live chat with real opinions, and makes for genuinely entertaining hosting content. The 3-host judging panel is gold — lean into the disagreement.

How the giveaway works

1
Subscribe to The Kennel on YouTube. Hard gate — no subscribe, no entry. Lead with this in every post.
2
Drop your hottest Bulldogs vs Cowboys take in the comments on the announcement post. Bold prediction, spicy opinion, funniest take — whatever you've got.
3
Tune in Tuesday at 8pm. The 3 hosts read the best takes live and pick the winner on air. You don't have to be watching to win — but you'd be mad to miss it.
4
Optional bonus entry: Share the giveaway post to your Instagram story and tag The Kennel. Counts as a second entry — expands reach beyond existing audience.

Framing advice

Counter the "I'm not funny enough" objection by emphasising BOLDNESS alongside comedy. "Hottest take", "most controversial opinion", "boldest prediction" — broadens who feels like they can enter. Don't make it feel like a comedy competition only.
On-stream suggestion: One host plays hard to impress, one plays softie, one is the wildcard. Read the top 8–10 takes, debate them like a panel show, then crown the winner. This is the most entertaining version of a giveaway you can run — and the judging segment is a standalone highlight clip afterward.
